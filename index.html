My Story

I have been around computers since a young age. At age 11 I learned HTML 4 to make awful 90s Pokémon websites complete with flashing gifs and the <marquee> tag. I later made popular game mods for various games popular in the early 2000s.

In 2010 I graduated from Texas A&M University with a degree in Civil Engineering. My specialty was simulating traffic, rainfall, and runoff patterns to help build sustainable communities.

Upon graduation I was commissioned as a US Army Corps of Engineer officer. I spent six years as a supervisor and project manager on various engineering projects around the world. I have led teams as small as 3 and as large as 180. I served as lead on $18 million-dollar projects involving 400 people and made decisions that permanently impacted people’s lives. I know what it means to be a servant leader and how to forge the next generation of leaders to replace me.

I have always maintained a game development hobby and have used C# and Unity3D since 2015. After I finished my Army career I decided to pivot and turn my hobby into a career by becoming a Software Engineer.
